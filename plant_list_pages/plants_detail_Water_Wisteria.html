Description

Hygrophila difformis, commonly known as Water Wisteria, is a popular aquatic plant valued for its lush foliage and graceful appearance. It features delicate, feathery leaves that are typically green in color, though they can take on reddish hues under certain conditions. The plant has a bushy growth habit with multiple stems branching out from a central point.





Distribution

Hygrophila difformis is native to the Indian subcontinent, specifically found in countries like India, Bangladesh, and Nepal. It grows in various aquatic environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams.
